Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) have emerged as a powerful paradigm for image synthesis and transformation tasks within the domain of computer vision. Their ability to learn complex data distributions enables the generation of highly realistic images. This project presents a landmark-guided facial image generation framework based on a Conditional Generative Adversarial Network (cGAN).

The proposed approach utilizes facial landmark heatmaps as structured conditioning inputs, which provide spatial constraints and guide the generation process toward producing semantically consistent facial features. A U-Net based generator architecture is employed to effectively capture both global and local spatial information through skip connections, while a PatchGAN discriminator is used to enforce local realism and texture consistency in generated outputs.

In addition to adversarial and reconstruction losses, perceptual loss is incorporated to enhance high-level feature representation, thereby improving the visual quality and structural coherence of generated images. The experimental results demonstrate that integrating spatial conditioning with adversarial learning significantly improves control over facial structure and enhances the realism of generated images.
